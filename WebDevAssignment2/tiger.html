The Tiger

The Tiger is the largest species in the cat family.

Here are some facts about tigers: Size: Males can grow up to 10.5 feet long and can weigh between 450 and 675 pounds. Females are up to 8 feet long and weigh 200 to 350 pounds. Lifespan: Estimated lifespan of 10 to 15 years in the wild, and an average lifespan of 16 to 22 years in human care. Color: Reddish-orange to reddish-brown with distinctive dark vertical stripes. Range: The Amur River Valley in the Russian Far East, in Northern China, and Southern Russia in Asia. They no longer inhabit Siberia. Habitat: Primarily found in woodlands and birch forests.
